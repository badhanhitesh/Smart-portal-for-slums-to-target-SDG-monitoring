The phrase "Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient and sustainable" is part of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 11, which was adopted by 193 member countries in 2015 as part of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development. The goal aims to ensure that all people have access to safe and affordable housing, adequate basic services, and opportunities for social, economic and cultural development.
The mission of slum development is to improve the living conditions and quality of life for people living in slums, which are often characterized by inadequate housing, overcrowding, lack of basic services and infrastructure, and poverty. This mission can be accomplished through a combination of interventions.
The SDG 11 aims to create cities and human settlements that are inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable, so that all people can live in dignity and prosperity, and the planet can continue to support human life for generations to come.
